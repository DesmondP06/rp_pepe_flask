The Growth Inhibiting Effects of Indole-3-Carbinol (I3C)
and N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) on E. coli cells

An In-Vitro Study

Historical Persepective

  • Bacterial resistance to traditional synthetic antibiotics is on the rise due to overpresciption

  • Plant materials have been shown to be promising alternatives to synthetic antibacterial compounds

  • Indole-3-Carbinol (I3C) has been shown to exhibit fungicidal and bactericidal behavior by increasing cellular ROS

  • N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) has previously been assessed for its ability to break up bacterial biofilms

Research Goal and Hypothesis

  • This study aimed to elucidate the effects of I3C and NAC on the growth and survivability of E. coli cultures

  • Because I3C and NAC have previously exhibited antimicrobial and antibacterial properties, and the introduction of these compounds into different types of bacteria has resulted in apoptosis-like cell death, we believed there would be a dose-dependent effect on the growth and survivability of E. coli when these compounds were introduced in different concentrations.

  • Filter disks containing solutions with different concentrations of these two compounds were introduced to E. coli cultures

  • NAC did not have an affect on the growth or survivability of E. coli

  • I3C had a dose-dependant effect on the growth and survivability of E. coli
